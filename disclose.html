Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

I believe I've found a vulnerability

First; thank you for taking the time to look up this policy page.
Second; please contact me with details on the vulnerability and what you believe could be done to mitigate it.

Once I've recieved your message, I will take a few days to verify and understand the vulnerability. During this time I might also have to conduct triage or implement temporary safeguards, depending on how serious the vulnerability is. I will then write back to you to acknowledge I've recieved the message and what I plan to do next.

Once I've resolved the vulnerability I will contact you again to confirm the vulnerability is no longer present. Once the vulnerability has been confirmed to no longer be present, I will offer you my thanks and a spot on my acknowledgement page.

I make every effort to resolve vulnerabilities, assuming they are within my power to resolve. If I believe the vulnerability is outside my area of control, I will thank you for the disclosure and try my best to point you to others that are in a position to resolve it.

If you need to contact me with especially sensitive information, please encrypt your message with PGP. Here is a command to import my public PGP key (remember to verify!): 

curl https://www.stylback.se/key.asc | gpg --import

I would like to poke around for vulnerabilities, is that OK?

Yes, I do however ask three things of you:

  1. That you act in good faith.
  2. That you do not use methods that harm, damage, or disrupts my services nor use methods that are illegal. These methods include DoS/DDoS:ing my server, trying to phis me or physically breaking into my home.
  3. That if you do find a vulnerability, you contact me so that I get an opportunity to try and fix it.

I might've broken something, am I in trouble?

If you've acted in good faith, I assure you that I will not be making any complaints to law enforcement or otherwise.

Accidents happens and are a part of the learning process. I would rather that you accidentally break things in good faith today than someone else intentionally breaking them in bad faith tomorrow.

If something were to happen, I urge you to contact me with an honest description of the event and we will take it from there.

I have concerns or questions with regards to this policy

I would love to hear it, please send me an email and we can take it from there.
